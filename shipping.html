Packing Tips for Safe Delivery

1

Choose the Right Box

Box should be slightly larger than item. Fill empty space with padding. Reused Amazon boxes work great!

2

Wrap Fragile Items

Bubble wrap for fragile items. Newspaper, old clothes, or air pillows for padding. Double-box very fragile items.

3

Seal It Securely

Use strong packing tape. Tape all seams and edges. The "H" pattern is strongest: across top, bottom, and down the middle.

4

Label Clearly

We provide the shipping label - just print and stick it on. Cover any old labels or barcodes. One label per package.

5

Take Photos

Before sealing, take photos of the packed item. This protects both you and your trade partner if issues arise.

6

Track Your Package

We email you tracking info automatically. Both parties can see real-time updates in the app.

Shipping FAQs

Do I need a printer?

Nope! Most UPS and FedEx locations can print your label from a QR code. Just show your phone. USPS also accepts QR codes at most locations.

What if I don't have a box?

UPS Stores and FedEx Office sell boxes (usually $2-5). Staples has a huge selection. Or grab free boxes from grocery stores, liquor stores, or friends!

Can I schedule a pickup?

Yes! USPS offers free home pickup when you schedule online. UPS and FedEx charge $4-6 for home pickup. Premium members get pickup discounts.

How long does shipping take?

Typically 3-5 business days for ground shipping. Express options available for faster delivery (2-day or overnight) at additional cost.

Is shipping insured?

Yes! All shipments include up to $100 insurance automatically. You can add more insurance for high-value items (usually $1 per $100 of value).

What if my package is lost or damaged?

We'll file a claim with the carrier. If approved, you get the insured value. If denied, we reverse the trade and return items to original owners.
